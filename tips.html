IITs, NITs & Engineering Colleges (JEE)

  • Focus heavily on JEE Mains and Advanced — your score is everything.
  • Boards matter only for eligibility (around 75%), not for selection.
  • Coaching helps, but self-discipline is key — mock tests every week.
  • Use resources like Allen modules, PW, or NTA sample papers.
  • Holistic skills or extracurriculars don’t carry much weight here.

Private Universities (BITS, VIT, SRM, etc.)

  • Entrance exams like BITSAT, VITEEE, SRMJEE are your ticket in.
  • English, reasoning, and math are equally important.
  • Boards or personal interviews have minor impact — exam rank rules.
  • Some offer direct admission for top board students.
  • Prepare smartly — these exams test speed, not just depth.

Abroad Universities (US, UK, Canada)

  • Focus on academics, essays, and extracurricular balance.
  • SAT/ACT scores still matter for scholarships and admissions.
  • Build your profile early — internships, volunteering, and projects help.
  • Personal essays show personality — write authentically.
  • Get strong recommendation letters from teachers who actually know you.

New-Age Colleges (Scaler, Newton School, Masai, etc.)

  • They prioritize skill over scores — your coding or portfolio matters more.
  • No traditional entrance exams — expect aptitude + interview rounds.
  • Showcase GitHub projects, hackathons, or tech blog contributions.
  • Stay consistent with personal branding on LinkedIn or X (Twitter).
  • Be ready to explain your motivation and career goals clearly.
